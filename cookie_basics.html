What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites store on a user‚Äôs computer or mobile device. These files contain **key-value pairs** of information that help websites remember user preferences, authentication details, and tracking data.

Why Do Websites Use Cookies?

  • Personalization: Websites use cookies to remember user settings, such as language preferences and theme choices.
  • Authentication: Websites use session cookies to keep users logged in, so they don't have to re-enter their passwords every time they visit.
  • Analytics & Tracking: Websites track user behavior to improve services and show relevant advertisements.

Common Types of Cookies

Type Description
Session Cookies Deleted when the browser is closed; used for temporary storage.
Persistent Cookies Stored on the device for a set duration, even after closing the browser.
Third-Party Cookies Created by websites other than the one you're visiting (e.g., for advertising).

Are Cookies Safe?

Cookies themselves are **not inherently dangerous**, but they can be misused. **Tracking cookies** and **third-party cookies** are often used to track user behavior across websites, which raises privacy concerns.

How to Manage Cookies

  • Clear cookies regularly from your browser.
  • Block third-party cookies in browser settings.
  • Use browser extensions to manage cookie permissions.
