Are Cookies Safe?

Cookies themselves are not dangerous, but they can be misused by hackers if websites don‚Äôt secure them properly. This can lead to stolen passwords, tracking without consent, or unauthorized access to accounts.

How Hackers Can Steal Cookies

Hackers use different tricks to steal cookies. Here are some common ways:

  • Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Attackers inject harmful code into a website to steal cookies.
  • Session Hijacking: If a hacker gets your session cookie, they can pretend to be you.
  • Unencrypted Cookies: If cookies are sent over HTTP instead of HTTPS, they can be intercepted.
  • Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF): Attackers trick you into making unwanted actions on websites.

How Websites Keep Cookies Safe

To protect cookies, websites use security features like:

  • Secure Cookies: Cookies are only sent over HTTPS to prevent theft.
  • HttpOnly Cookies: These cannot be accessed by JavaScript, stopping XSS attacks.
  • SameSite Attribute: Stops cookies from being sent in cross-site requests, reducing CSRF attacks.
  • Short Expiration Times: Websites delete session cookies after a short time to reduce risks.

How You Can Protect Yourself

Even if websites secure cookies, you can take extra steps to protect your data:

  • Use HTTPS websites whenever possible.
  • Clear cookies regularly in your browser settings.
  • Use private browsing mode if you don‚Äôt want cookies saved.
  • Block third-party cookies to reduce tracking.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for extra security.
